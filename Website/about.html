Brief Introduction

In the Home section, you learned about how I was involved on campus at Northeastern! In this section, you'll learn about my music life, which was before college!

Even though I consider myself an aspiring data scientist, I have always been a classical musician. I've been playing the piano since I was 5 years old, and I started learning the cello at 11 years old. So if you do the math, I've been playing the piano for almost 15 years and the cello for 8 years!! Although I can proudly say this, this was never easy, as I went to so many private lessons growing up as a kid. While it's true that I may have been forced by my parents as a child, my decision to continue playing these instruments are now at my own will, as music makes up my identity. Without music, I don't even know how I would have gone through the toughest moments of my life.

Brief Rundown of My Music Background

I have had the blessing of going to a performing arts high school in New York City where I majored in both piano and cello!

The opportunities I was able to have from this school felt so surreal at times. I've had the opportunity to perform a many venues across Lincoln Center! Like I said earlier, this process was never easy, but looking back on it, I don't regret a second of it because it has shaped me into the person I am today. Even though I had no interest in becoming a professional musician in high school, I'm so glad of the valuable lessons I've learned.
